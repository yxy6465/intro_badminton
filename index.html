What is badminton?

Badminton is a racquet sport played using rackests to hint the shuttlecock by two side of people. It has become a popular sport not only as a fun sport which can be played among people but also as a competitive sport match. In usual, a match game is played in singles(one player per side)/doubles(two players as a team for each side). As the fastest raqucet sport in the world, a shuttle can reach a unbelievalbe speed of 332km/h by a strong smashing. Becasue of the speciality of badminton, a intense game in competition always entertain and excite the audiance. And playing badminton can be challenging and exctiing.

History

The origins of the game of badminton can be dated back to the game of battledore and shuttlecock in ancient Greece China, and India probably at least 2000 years ago. In the 1600s, battledore and shuttlecock was an upper class in England and other European countries. As the development of this sport, about 1893, a game is named for Badmintion was played by the country estate of the dukes of Beaufort in Gloucestershire, England.

"The Badminton World Federation (BWF; originally the International Badminton Federation), the world governing body of the sport, was formed in 1934. The best-known of these is the All-England Championships. Other well-known international tournaments include the Thomas Cup (donated 1939) for men’s team competition and the Uber Cup (donated 1956) for women’s team competition."(Encyclopædia Britannica, inc.,2019)

Throughout the history, only five countries have won gold medals at the Olympics since badminton was introduced in 1992 - China, Indonesia and Korea, England and Denmark.

Benefit of playing badminton

1. Improve the function of the whole body. For example, reducing high blood pressure, heart health issues and cervical vertebra problems, decreasing the rate of diabetes, strengthen the heart and lung's functionalities, etc.

2. Build up the shape of the body. Since badminton will make you run, especially when you have a competitive match. The movements while playing badminton will help to build and tone your core muscles especially for calves, butt, quads and hamstrings.

3. Help with social relationships. Since you cannot play badminton on your own. As a social sport, you will be able to meet with new people and talk to your friends. Teamwork in doubles matches is very important!

4. Help to lose weight in a healthy way. This is a sport that makes you run in order to hit the birdie, you are able to burn close to 480 calories in an hour in a competitive game.

5. Help with poor eyesight and slow reflexes. After watching the video above, now you know how intense a game can be. Since badminton is a fast sport, in order to catch up the fast birdie, you have to focus. At that time, your eye will get relax from daily usage and refresh your nerves to improve your reflexes.
