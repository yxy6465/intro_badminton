Goal of playing badminton

The goal of playing a badminton game is to try to hit the shuttlecock to your oppoiste court area so that your opponents are not able to hit back. The basic rule is that the shuttlecock has to be hitted over the net and does not hit to the area that is out of the court. Based on above, badminton is a sport that not only tests a player's physical abilities but also tests the player's mental abilities. In the field, a successful player needs to consider the movement of her opponents, the best spot to hit the shuttle to, the next stratedy of next rally, and so on. More information will be presented at the following sections.

Shuttlecock: feather vs. plastic

Shuttlecock, also called as shuttle/birdie, is an essential equipment in badminton. It has an open conical shape. The main components of the shuttlecock are the round cock (made of Natural rubber) and feathers. There are two types of shuttle are commonly used in the game: feather shuttle and nylon shuttle. In usual, a shuttle is a samll cork hemisphere with 16 goose feathers attached and with weight of 5 grams. And this type of shuttle is used in official game. However, what is the main difference between these two shuttles?

  • Less durable. Play a match and you might need 2-3 shuttles at least.
  • Lighter. It's eaiser to control by the users, so the shuttles go exactly where you hit them. Trick shot will be easier to use.
  • More durable. One nylon shuttle can be used for 10 matches or more.
  • Heavier. Upon hitting their trajectory deviates a bit. But usually with a faster speed.

Basic skills

Types of game

Singles

  • Men's Singles/Women's Singles.
  • The singles court is narrower than doubles court, but same length.
  • Since one person needs to cover the entire court, singles game are based on forcing the opponent to move as much as possible. Therefore, more trick shots will happen and the tempo of game will be a little slower than doubles.

Doubles

  • Men's Doubles/Women's Doubles.
  • The line rules of doubles game are different from the rules of singles game. It's larger.
  • Both pairs will try to maintain the attack, hitting downwards by smashing, dropping and driving as much as possible.
  • While attacking, one player will hit down from the rear-court to wait to kill the game, the other player will try to smash and drop to provide wining chances. While defending, two player will stay at the same level side by side in order to enhance the range to defend.
  • The tempo is usually faster than singles game.

Mixed Doubles

  • A pair with one male player and one female player.
  • In mixed doubles, both pairs try to maintain an attacking formation with the woman at the front and the man at the back to maximize the ability to attack.
  • The tempo is slower than men's doubles and faster than women's doubles. In usual, mixed doubles will amaze the audience by some incredible combo of attack.

Scoring System

  • A point is scored when you hit the birdie over the net and land it in your opponent's court before she hits it.
  • In current system, to win a game, you are required to win 2 out of 3 sets of game. For each set, you must gain 21 points before your opponent. If the socres tie at 20-20, duce happens, and you must win 2 points in a row to win the set.
  • Touching the net with your body or racket results in a point of conceding.
